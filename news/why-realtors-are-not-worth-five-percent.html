Why Realtors Aren't Worth 5% in 2026

The real estate industry hasn't changed its commission model in decades. Meanwhile, every tool an agent uses is now available to homeowners for a fraction of the cost.

In 1990, a real estate agent was your only gateway to the market. They had the MLS book, the network, and the negotiation experience. The 5% commission — split between buyer's and seller's agents — was the cost of access.

It's 2026. You have more data on your phone than an agent had in their entire office 30 years ago. So why are we still paying like it's 1990?

The tools have changed. The fee hasn't.

Consider what's available to every Canadian homeowner today:

  • MLS access — Flat-fee listing services put you on Realtor.ca for $200–$500. Same exposure, fraction of the cost.
  • Pricing data — AI-powered comp analysis is more accurate than an agent pulling three "similar" listings from memory.
  • Photography — Professional real estate photographers charge $200–$400 directly. No middleman markup.
  • Legal protection — Your real estate lawyer handles all contracts, title transfer, and compliance. They always have, with or without an agent.
  • Marketing — Social media, targeted ads, and listing syndication are available to anyone.

What you're really paying for

When you pay 5% commission on a $500,000 home, you're handing over $25,000. For that money, your agent will:

  1. Hire a photographer (cost to them: $300)
  2. Enter your listing into MLS (cost to them: $0 — it's included in their brokerage fees)
  3. Put a sign on your lawn
  4. Schedule showings (calendar invites)
  5. Relay offers between buyer and seller
  6. Fill in standard AREA forms

That's it. The legal work? Lawyer. The financing? Mortgage broker. The inspection? You hire that directly. The title insurance? Your lawyer arranges it.

The liability argument doesn't hold up

Agents love to say they "protect" you in the transaction. But at closing, your agent carries zero liability. If something goes wrong with the title, the contract, or the funds — that's between you, your lawyer, and the other party's lawyer.

Agent E&O (Errors & Omissions) insurance exists, but claims are rare and payouts are rarer. RECA complaints in Alberta rarely result in meaningful consequences. The system protects agents, not consumers.

The alternative is already here

Private home sales are 100% legal in every Canadian province. Thousands of Canadians sell privately every year. With modern tools — AI pricing, flat-fee MLS, and a real estate lawyer — you get better service for $1,700 instead of $25,000.

The question isn't whether you can sell without a realtor. It's why you'd choose to give away $25,000 when you don't have to.
